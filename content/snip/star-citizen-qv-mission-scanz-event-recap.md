# Star Citizen QV Mission Report — Shenaniscanz Edition

What does a **Star Citizen QV mission** actually look like in an ANZ community?

Well… tonight’s run kicked off with what historians will surely call *“an educational experiment in ship durability.”*

---

## Mission Brief (Star Citizen QV Event)

- **Objective:** QV site run  
- **Location:** Nyx (allegedly functional)  
- **Ships involved:** Cutlass Black, Asgard, various regrettable vehicle choices  
- **Outcome:** Partial success, total chaos  

---

## What Happened During the QV Mission

CeePeeNz decided it would be a brilliant scientific endeavour to test the structural integrity of a Cutlass Black and an Asgard.

No shields.  
Full boost.  
NAV mode.  
Directly into the rusty sheriff badge of the Cutlass.

**Result:**  
BIG ARSE EXPLOSION.  
Science was achieved. Ships were not.

---

After regrouping and collecting what was left of our dignity, we pushed into the QV site and had a few *friendly shooty chats* with the loitering Vanduul.

During this time, Saint became permanently emotionally attached to a ship seat and couldn’t get out. Eventually he wriggled free and we continued our little *shoot and root*—*I mean, meet and greet* with the locals.

Alarm system: off.  
Data files: acquired.  
Confidence: misplaced.

---

Area A worked beautifully.  
Area B worked like a wet sock.

Undeterred by reality, we pushed on to the **Busted-Arse Polaris**, grabbed more data, and collected a few warehouse codes.

Spirits were high. Things were looking promising.

---

## Star Citizen Chaos: The Motorcycle Phase

Then we became Nyx’s shittest space motorcycle gang.

All the gear.  
No idea.

People got stuck on bikes inside a hangar elevator—which is impressive considering the elevator barely works for walking humans.

At this exact moment, **Daddy Roberts** activated the traditional *SERVER ERROR EXPERIENCE™*, and we all stood around contemplating life while the server rebooted its brain.

---

Eventually we got moving again toward the AUX base, but the chaos escalated:

- People couldn’t mount bikes  
- Bikes mounted people  
- Physics quietly resigned  

Then Deadpool—also known as *The Duber*—heroically flew in and airlifted a few stranded souls out of the mess.

Meanwhile, Synikk tested the impact resistance of a Dragonfly by flying it full pelt into the outer wall of the asteroid base.

**Results:** Conclusive. 🧨

---

## When the Mission Breaks (Classic Star Citizen)

Next step: locate the **Laptop of Mild Importance**.

We found it.  
Grabbed the code: **6999**  
Ran back to the door.  
Punched it in…

**ACCESS DENIED.**

Apparently the system had correctly identified us as SCANZ scallywags.

---

## The Only Thing That Worked

Then Subodhi had the greatest idea of the evening:

**Yeet a Pulse bike at Skiddies.**

Which—and this cannot be stressed enough—was the **only thing that worked all mission.**

In went Skiddies.  
We grabbed the fourth data file and the third warehouse code.  
And somehow… the Star Citizen mission progressed.

---

## Mission Outcome (Somehow Successful)

We made it back to the main base like semi-victorious idiots.

- Warehouse opened  
- Loot acquired  
- Ship components adopted into loving homes  

For a **Star Citizen QV mission**, we’re calling that a win.

---

## Final Thoughts on Star Citizen Group Gameplay

Despite explosions, broken elevators, suicidal motorcycles, and Daddy Roberts actively sabotaging the space cowboy & co… *(and the fact the mission itself was completely borked)*…

The crew had a great time.

And honestly—that’s what **Star Citizen group gameplay** is all about.

For ANZ players especially, having a consistent crew turns even broken missions into great nights.

Another classic SCANZ adventure in the books.
